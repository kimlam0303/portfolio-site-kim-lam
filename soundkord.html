Project 1 - SoundKord

SoundKord is an online store specifying on microphones but it is also offering various other studio gear, such as headphones, monitor speakers etc. For this project I went through the process of branding, creating the user-flow, wireframing and creating mockups for multiple breakpoints.

My Process

Branding - Colors

Contrary to the “traditional“ approach to new projects, the first task for me was to establish a fitting brand for this concept. Going into this I already had a general idea of how the aesthetic should feel like - tech-oriented, minimal, professional, yet pleasing to the look at. Based on these guiding principles, I decided on these deep and modern colors for the product.

Branding - Typography

For the typeface I have decided to use Nunito for headlines and subheadlines. The body text is written in Montserrat. Regarding the writing style, the language we use is rather simple, yet precise. It is not overwhelming customers with too much technical jargon, but is instead easy, friendly and professional.

User Experience - User Flow

To figure out what screens I needed to design, I created a user flow, upon which I determined the necessity of each screen. After some iterations I came up with the final result, seen above.

User Testing & Feedback

Before proceeding with the HiFi wireframes, I decided to test the flow on actual users. The scenario I’ve given my testers was to purchase an item directly (without going to the basket), through the search feature on the homepage. Apart from having them give feedback on the intuitiveness of the flow - or lack thereof - I have also encouraged them to think of any suggestions regarding the design.
